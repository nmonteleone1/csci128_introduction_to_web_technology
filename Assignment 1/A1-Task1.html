Sweet Chilli Loaf
 🌶 🌶
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 6 sprigs parsley
  • 450g water
  • 750g flour
  • 1.5tsp salt
  • 4tsp yeast
  • Cheese, to taste
  • Sweet chilli sauce, to taste
  1. Place cubed cheese into Thermomix bowl and chop for 6 seconds/speed 9; set aside.
  2. Without rinsing bowl, place garlic and parsley into Thermomix and chop for 5 seconds/speed 7.
  3. Add all other Bread ingredients into the bowl, including a little of the grated cheese. Mix for 5 secs/speed 7 to combine.
  4. With dial set to Closed lid, Knead for 2 minutesdough mode
  5. Preheat oven to 180 degrees
  6. Let dough rise for 30 minutes Or until doubled in size.
  7. Roll onto Thermomat in a large rectangle. Spread with sweet chilli sauce and sprinkle with reserved grated cheese.
  8. With a spatula, cut down the centre and then into strips 3-4cm wide. Fold each dough strip into a concertina pattern and place cut side up in a 26cm springform tin Until all strips are placed in together. Top with any leftover cheese.
  9. Bake for 40-45 minutes.
Chilli-Chocolate Mousse
 🌶 🌶 🌶 🌶
  • 250g dark Lindor chocolate balls
  • 600mL thickened cream
  • 250g chillis (seeds removed)
  1. Blend chillis with a stick blender and 50mL of cream until homogenous.
  2. Reserve 6 of the Lindor balls. Unwrap the remaining balls and place in a heatproof bowl place over a saucepan of simmering water, along with ½ cup of the cream (don’t let the water touch the base of the bowl).
  3. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 5 minutes or until chocolate has melted. Add chilli cream and stir to combine. Set aside to cool slightly.
  4. Use electric beaters to beat the remaining cream until firm peaks form. Transfer about 2/3 of a cup of the whipped cream to a separate bowl and place in fridge until required.
  5. Add about 2 tbs of the remaining unrefrigerated whipped cream to the melted Lindor ball mixture. Stir to loosen slightly. Add the Lindor mixture to the remaining cream and beat gently until combined. Divide among 6 serving glasses. Cover and place in the fridge for 2 hours to chill. Serve topped with reserved 2/3 cup cream and Lindor balls.
Spicy Garlic Flatbread
 🌶 🌶 🌶
  • 375mL plain yoghurt
  • 30mL oil
  • 330g self raising flour
  • 30g butter
  • 2 cloves garlic (crushed)
  • 1/2tsp dried chilli flakes
  1. Combine all the ingredients in a bowl and mix with a fork.
  2. Turn out onto a lightly floured board and gathered together to form a soft dough.
  3. Leave to rest for 10 minutes
  4. While the dough rests, make the garlic butter by melting butter with 2 cloves of crushed garlic. Add chilli flakes
  5. Simmer on low heat until the butter is melted and remove from the heat.
  6. Divide the dough into 10 to 12 pieces, then roll each out into a circle around
  7. Heat a pan over medium/high heat.
  8. Cook for 30 seconds on each side.When you will see little bubbles rise on the surface, flip the flatbreads over.
  9. You will see that the flatbreads puffs up slightly and it will get little brown spots as it cooks.
  10. Remove from the heat and brush both sides with the garlic butter. Cover with a clean kitchen towel or paper towel as you cook the rest of the flatbreads
Spicy Baked Chicken Breast
 🌶 🌶 🌶
  • 1kg chicken breast
  • 25mL olive oil
  • 1tsp garlic powder
  • 1tsp onion powder
  • 1tsp dried oregano
  • 1tsp dried thyme
  • 0.5tsp cayenne pepper
  • 0.5tsp black pepper
  • 1.5tsp paprika
  • 0.5tsp salt
  1. Add all spices and store in an airtight container. (Creole Seasoning)
  2. Preheat oven to 400 degrees.
  3. Prepare a baking dish by coating the bottom with half the olive oil. Use the remaining 1/2 tablespoon coat the inside of one parchment paper sheet.
  4. Rub creole seasoning on both sides of each chicken breast and place in the prepared dish.
  5. Cover with parchment paper (olive oil side down) and tuck paper around edges of chicken so it is as airtight as possible.
  6. Bake 40 to 45 minutes, until a meat thermometer inserted in the center reads at least 165 degrees.
Lime Grilled Shrimp
 🌶 🌶 🌶
  • 3tbsp Cajun seasoning
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 1tbsp vegetable oil
  • 0.5kg shrimp
  1. Mix together the Cajun seasoning, lime juice, and vegetable oil in a resealable plastic bag. Add the shrimp, coat with the marinade, squeeze out excess air, and seal the bag. Marinate in the refrigerator for 20 minutes.
  2. Preheat an outdoor grill for medium heat, and lightly oil the grate. Remove the shrimp from the marinade, and shake off excess. Discard the remaining marinade.
  3. Cook the shrimp on the preheated grill until they are bright pink on the outside and the meat is no longer transparent in the center, about 2 minutes per side.
Marinara Sauce
 🌶 🌶
  • 3tbsp olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 1 can crushed tomato
  • 0.5cup red wine
  • 0.5cup water
  • 1tsp salt
  • 1tsp white sugar
  • 1tsp chilli flakes
  • 6 basil leaves, torn
  1. Heat oil in a large non-stick skillet over low heat and saute garlic for about 2 minutes; be careful not to burn.
  2. Just as the garlic begins to turn brown, add chilli flakes and remove pan from heat.
  3. Allow pan to cool, and add tomatoes, wine, water, salt and sugar. Cook over medium-high heat and bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat to low and simmer, covered, about 20 minutes.
  5. Remove from heat and stir in basil.
Peanut Butter Cookies
 🌶
  • 200g peanut butter
  • 125g caster sugar
  • 0.25tsp salt
  • 1 egg
  1. Heat oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4 and line 2 large baking trays with baking parchment.
  2. Measure the peanut butter and sugar into a bowl. Add ¼ tsp fine table salt and mix well with a wooden spoon. Add the egg and mix again until the mixture forms a dough.
  3. Break off cherry tomato sized chunks of dough and place, well spaced apart, on the trays. Press the cookies down with the back of a fork to squash them a little. The cookies can now be frozen for 2 months, cook from frozen adding an extra min or 2 to the cooking time.
  4. Bake for 12 mins, until golden around the edges and paler in the centre. Cool on the trays for 10 mins, then transfer to a wire rack and cool completely. Store in a cookie jar for up to 3 days.
